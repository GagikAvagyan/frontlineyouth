What we do

Programs that move ideas into action.

Our initiatives reflect the diversity of Frontline's work - from educational programs and participatory research to community engagement and resource development. Explore the projects that continue to shape our impact and inspire new opportunities for collaboration.

Since 2020, Frontline has been advancing peace education in Armenia by combining research, educational resource development, and teacher professional development. We create practical tools that help educators introduce peace, conflict, and dialogue into the classroom while supporting broader educational reforms. Our goal is to make peace education accessible, relevant, and transformative for both teachers and young people.

From 2021 to 2025, Seed the Change brought together young people from across Armenia for an immersive summer school experience centered on peacebuilding, environmental sustainability, and human rights. Through interactive learning, outdoor activities, creative methods, and collaborative projects, participants developed practical skills and lasting connections.

The experience continues through the Seed the Change Activist Handbook, which supports participants in turning ideas into action.

What began as a creative exercise during the Seed the Change summer school evolved into a nationwide participatory research initiative exploring how young people imagine their future. Through focus groups and artistic expression, Frontline engaged diverse youth communities to better understand identity, belonging, and aspirations.

The findings were transformed into a traveling exhibition, creating space for dialogue and amplifying young people's voices.

Peace Academy is a two-month experiential learning program that continues the journey begun through the Seed the Change Summer School. Designed for university students, it offers a deeper exploration of conflict, peace, and social change through an interactive, non-formal learning environment.

Participants strengthen their understanding of conflict and peace studies while developing practical skills in dialogue, collaboration, negotiation, and conflict transformation. More than a course, the Academy is a learning community where knowledge is co-created through discussion, reflection, and shared experience.

Building on our participatory research, this initiative explores how peace, conflict, and trauma can be addressed through safe, inclusive, and creative learning environments. By bringing together educators, researchers, and practitioners, we developed practical trauma-informed teaching approaches grounded in international expertise and local experience.

The result is a collection of educational methods that support more responsive and resilient classrooms.

Learn more →

PeaceON is an animated podcast created for young people, bringing complex social topics to life through a simple, engaging, and modern format. Guided by Piso, the curious cat and main character of the podcast, young audiences explore topics ranging from disinformation and media literacy to environmental issues, financial literacy, and everyday choices.

Through real stories, relatable examples, and honest conversations, PeaceON encourages young people to think critically, stay informed, and become active participants in their communities.

The learning journey continues through the PeaceON app, where users can reflect on episodes, explore new topics, take interactive quizzes, test their knowledge, and receive certificates upon successful completion.

Mobile app

Peace ON

Peace ON is an educational app that helps students learn and assess their knowledge of peacebuilding through interactive content, readings, and tests.

The content is based on the Peace Education handbook for the Social Science school curriculum, confirmed by the Ministry of Education of Armenia in 2022.
